# 📚 Further Reading & Reference Resources

To deepen your understanding of FinOps, systems performance engineering, and Kubernetes scheduling, review the following curated resources.

---

## 1. Books and Technical Guides

*   **Cloud FinOps (2nd Edition)** by J.R. Storment and Mike Fuller (O'Reilly)
    *   *The definitive textbook on cloud cost management, culture, and framework operations.*
*   **Systems Performance (2nd Edition)** by Brendan Gregg (Addison-Wesley)
    *   *Essential reading for systems engineering, detailing Linux kernels, performance tools, and capacity planning.*
*   **Kubernetes in Action** by Marko Lukša (Manning)
    *   *Specifically, review Chapter 14: Managing Pod Resources for an in-depth understanding of resource limits.*

---

## 2. Industry Specifications and Projects

*   **FinOps Foundation**: [https://www.finops.org/](https://www.finops.org/)
    *   *Official frameworks, certification guidelines, and case studies.*
*   **Karpenter Documentation**: [https://karpenter.sh/](https://karpenter.sh/)
    *   *Modern node provisioning, configuration schemas, and consolidation policies.*
*   **OpenCost**: [https://www.opencost.io/](https://www.opencost.io/)
    *   *The open-source specification and schema for measuring Kubernetes container and service costs.*
*   **Kubecost Product Blog**: [https://blog.kubecost.com/](https://blog.kubecost.com/)
    *   *Practical SRE-oriented posts on resizing, cost allocation, and multi-cloud billing.*
*   **Linux CFS Bandwidth Control**: [https://www.kernel.org/doc/Documentation/scheduler/sched-bwc.txt](https://www.kernel.org/doc/Documentation/scheduler/sched-bwc.txt)
    *   *Linux Kernel documentation explaining cgroups CPU quotas and periods.*
